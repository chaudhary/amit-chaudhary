About

A senior engineer you bring in when it has to work.

I've spent the last 14+ years building and architecting Ruby on Rails products across B2B and B2C — from greenfield builds to high-traffic systems that needed to stop falling over. I'm equally comfortable owning architecture decisions and getting my hands dirty in a messy, decade-old codebase.

These days I take the work most teams would rather not touch: version upgrades, performance rescue, scaling pain, and test-suite resurrection. I step into a codebase, make it boring and reliable again, and leave the team faster than I found them.

On AI: I have earned opinions on where coding agents genuinely speed you up versus where they quietly add risk — and how to keep a senior engineer in the loop. I've designed retrieval-grounded, agentic LLM systems end to end, not just prompted a chatbot.

What I do

Where I create the most value

Most of my engagements fall into one of these. All of them are the kind of work where experience pays for itself quickly.

Legacy Rails upgrades

Moving stuck apps across major Rails and Ruby versions safely — dependency hell, deprecations, and the test failures nobody wants to own.

Performance & scaling

Finding and killing the N+1s, slow queries, and architectural bottlenecks that show up when traffic grows. Postgres and Mongo tuning included.

Architecture & technical direction

Designing systems that stay simple as they grow. Making the high-stakes calls early so they don't become expensive rewrites later.

Codebase rescue

Stepping into inherited, under-tested, or abandoned codebases and making them reliable, shippable, and maintainable again.

AI-assisted development

Building LLM-backed features the right way — retrieval-grounded, evaluated, and shipped — plus helping teams use coding agents without shooting themselves in the foot.

Fractional senior / mentoring

Acting as the senior voice on a small team: reviewing, unblocking, mentoring, and raising the bar without the gatekeeping.
